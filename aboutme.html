About Me

Hi, I'm Nolan! I am currently a student at the University of Michigan-Dearborn, pursuing dual bachelor's degrees in Electrical and Computer Engineering.

My interest in engineering and electronics started when I was very young, taking apart everything I could and building simple circuits. When I was in middle school I joined a FIRST Robotics team at the FTC level, later moving up to FRC in high school where I eventually became the group lead for the Electrical group and later the Control Systems group. In the past I have also volunteered at various FIRST events and mentored students at the middle school level.

While working as an intern at Bosch, I have become familiar with the design and implementation of automotive/embedded electronics. I have gained experience in various aspects of electronic hardware design, including circuit design, PCB layout, worst-case circuit analysis, switching converter stability, and transient simulation. In addition to working with hardware, I have also done some software development using Python, MATLAB, and C++ (mostly for data analysis and visualization).
